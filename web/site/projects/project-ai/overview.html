The Problem

Every other alignment approach runs inside the model's privilege domain.

What everyone else does

Trust the model to govern itself.

RLHF, constitution prompts, chain-of-thought reflection — every major alignment technique today runs inside the same process as the model being aligned. If the model is compromised, so is the alignment layer. The guard and the prisoner share a cell.

Project-AI's position

Move governance to a layer the model can never reach.

The operating system kernel is structurally outside the model's privilege domain. eBPF programs attached to the kernel's syscall surface intercept every consequential action before it executes — not after, not concurrently, but before. Governance that happens at the kernel level cannot be overridden by anything running in user space.

"Capability without enforced constraint is theatre. The constraint must sit at a layer the actor cannot reach. For AI, that layer is the kernel."

— Jeremy Karrick, Project-AI System Paper · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.19582420
The Path

From nonprofit research to kernel-authoritative AGI governance.

Start

Public-sector researcher, no software background

Jeremy Karrick worked in nonprofit and public research — closest to journalism. No formal computer science training. No prior codebase. The work you see here was built from first principles, not from a CS degree.

Year 1

The constitutional question

The central question: what would it mean for an AI system to be governed by law rather than by preference? Not "helpful, harmless, honest" as training labels — but law, with enforcement, with record, with veto. The Triumvirate concept emerged from this framing.

Year 2

OctoReflex and the kernel insight

The insight that eBPF could intercept model actions at the syscall surface — before execution, in kernel space, at sub-millisecond latency — was the technical breakthrough that made the architecture non-fictional. OctoReflex became the containment engine.

21 Papers

A constitutional library, in writing

Every component of the architecture is documented in peer-reviewable, DOI-anchored papers on Zenodo. Not as documentation of existing software — as prior art, as a constitutional record, as a standing proof that this approach was conceived and written before anyone could claim otherwise.

Now

16 modules, a governance spine, and an open library

Project-AI is a live codebase with 16 named subsystems, 7,913 lines in the AEGIS core, 5 canonical invariants verified, and the library you are reading now. The work continues. The constitution is not finished — constitutions are never finished.

The Thesis

The 21-paper argument, in plain language.

Galahad

Ethics must be structural, not instructed.

A model told to be ethical can be told otherwise. A model whose syscalls are filtered by an ethics engine that runs in kernel space cannot override that filter — not through clever prompting, not through fine-tuning drift, not through any means available to user-space processes.

Cerberus

Security must be unfinishable, not just hard.

One bypass attempt spawns three new guardians. Exploitation is structurally recursive: the act of attacking the system makes it harder to attack. NIRL's reflex architecture means the threat surface shrinks as adversarial pressure increases.

CodexDeus

Law must be executable, not aspirational.

The AGI Charter is not a policy document — it compiles to deterministic checks via the Iron Path. T.A.R.L. rules are namespaced, immutable, and auditable at runtime. Thirsty-Lang is a programming language in which law is the only legal program.
