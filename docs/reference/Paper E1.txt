Closing the Misalignment Latency Window with a Kernel Reflex Layer in Agentic Systems
Abstract

Higher-tier governance systems inherently operate with non-zero detection-to-enforcement latency. We formalize the misalignment latency window and demonstrate how relocating enforcement below the policy engine eliminates it. Using eBPF LSM interception, containment becomes pre-syscall rather than post-detection. Architectural non-bypassability emerges from enforcement placement rather than model alignment quality.
