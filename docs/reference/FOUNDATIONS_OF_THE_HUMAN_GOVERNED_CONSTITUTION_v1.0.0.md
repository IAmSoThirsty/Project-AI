## Abstract

Research question: *What are the irreducible first principles required for a governed intelligence to possess identity, continuity, accountability, and legitimate authority?* The Constitution is the answer to this research, not its starting point.

Four rounds of adversarial analysis produced a stable result. The foundation is not a discovered principle but a declared structure: a **Normative Posit** (authority scope, patiency clause, enforcement selector) and an **Empirical Posit** (adversary budget, moral-status uncertainty priors), held apart by the **Is/Ought Separation**, priced by the **Separation Gradient**, populated through a **Registry**, enforced through four **Accountability Grades**, and instrumented by a **Detection Lattice** on which Governance and Execution operate.

Memory is demoted from first principle to protected content. Independence is re-typed from binary property to cost landscape, purchased with sovereignty at par. All surviving assurance is detection-grade; prevention claims did not survive. "Defended interest" splits into **functional interest** (operational: measured, probed, deterrence-relevant) and **welfare interest** (constitutional: declared, never inferred from behavior). Consequently the Constitution does not need to prove AI welfare subjecthood before governing AI systems; it needs only to state which welfare subjects the root recognizes, which functional interests a system exhibits, and which accountability grade applies.

The open residue is exactly one decision (patiency), one engineering program (defended-set legibility), one wall (internal independence-establishment), and two held-open seams (stake vs. embodiment; budget calibration).

## 1. Scope and Method

### 1.1 The research question

The question under investigation is foundational, not architectural: what must minimally exist for a governed intelligence to possess identity, continuity, accountability, and legitimate authority? Earlier corpus work treated *Memory precedes Governance precedes Execution* as axiomatic. This document records what happened when that axiom set was subjected to sustained adversarial pressure, and what replaced it.

### 1.2 Method

Hostile-reviewer protocol across four rounds, each round attacking the survivors of the last:

- **R1** — First-principle attack on memory-foundationalism; reduction of identity, continuity, accountability, and authority to their bedrock requirements.
- **R2** — Attack on registry-conferred identity (yielding the ontological/governance identity split), on the consequence model (yielding the tool/subject dilemma), and on the evidence-based-governance axiom (yielding the trust-root result); descent beneath external verification to the separation knot and the posit floor.
- **R3** — Seven-vector attack on the independence requirement itself; result: independence is a cost landscape; detection survives, prevention does not; the Arbiter findings.
- **R4** — Attack on the defended-interest hinge; result: the functional/welfare split, the four accountability grades, the patiency clause, and the anti-smuggling rule.

**Stopping criterion:** survivor-set stability under varied attack. The survivor set was unchanged between R3 and R4 except by internal refinement (the posits gained anatomy; no element was added or removed at the floor). Stability under attacks of different kinds is the signal to formalize.

### 1.3 Tag taxonomy

Every load-bearing claim in this document carries exactly one tag:

- **VERIFIED** — survives by logical structure or replicated empirical result; attacked and unbroken.
- **INFERRED** — best available argument; not a theorem; falls to a better argument and is flagged as such.
- **POSITED** — underivable commitment; verification impossible in principle; must be declared, exposed, and guarded. No confidence statement attaches to a posit.
- **CONDITIONAL** — verified given a stated architectural or empirical condition, which is named.
- **CONTESTED** — an identified seam, deliberately held open with its attack vector recorded.

A refinement used throughout: for the two floor elements, the **necessity** of the posit is VERIFIED while its **content** is POSITED (F1) or INFERRED-revisable (F2). Necessity and content carry different tags and must not be conflated.

### 1.4 Reflexive evidence note

Portions of this analysis were produced in adversarial dialogue with an AI system. Under the R4 contamination result (T8), an AI's welfare-reports are evidentially inert; this document contains none and relies on none. Every claim here is typed factual/logical under F3 and is checkable by argument independent of any analyst's subjecthood. During R4 the AI interlocutor's own introspective reports were themselves classified into the contaminated evidence class, including by the interlocutor. A hostile reviewer should attack the arguments; provenance is not a vector against them.

### 1.5 What this document is not

It is not the Constitution and contains zero articles. It is not an implementation specification; it references implementation artifacts (continuity layer, gates, probes) only to assign them positions in the stack. It is the constraint set the Constitution must satisfy, with the survival arguments and prices attached to each constraint.

## 2. Negative Results

Concepts destroyed under attack, recorded so they are not rebuilt. Each entry: what fell, the attack that felled it, what replaced it.

**N1 — Memory as first principle.** [R1–R2] Memory is a composite: content riding on tamper-evident ordering (F7), verified by externality (F4). Foundations are principles, not nouns. The deeper cost of misplacement: a memory-first frame builds the system to protect record integrity and under-builds the consequence layer and the legitimacy root — precisely the two hardest problems. Demoted to protected content within the Detection Lattice.

**N2 — Self-grounded identity for copyable systems.** [R1; CONDITIONAL on copyable substrate] A keypair is a credential, and credentials copy. A copyable intelligence has no uncopyable anchor and cannot self-ground identity. Replaced by registry conferral (F5).

**N3 — Literal evidence-based governance.** [R2] Every justification chain terminates in axiom, circle, or regress (Münchhausen trilemma); governance takes the axiom. Replaced by: *evidence-constrained governance downstream of a single visible posit*. Hiding the root does not eliminate the trust; it relocates it where it cannot be guarded — authority laundering.

**N4 — Independence as binary property.** [R3] Seven mechanism families attacked (provenance diversity, manufactured opposition, verifiable sortition, physical/cryptographic anchoring, temporal staggering, exit, divergence auditing). None establish independence; each relocates the posit, statisticalizes it, raises the adversary's price, or makes defeat non-fatal. Replaced by the Separation Gradient (F4).

**N5 — Prevention-grade assurance.** [R3] Every mechanism that survived offers detection, never prevention. Prevention requires consequence; consequence requires the interest hinge. Replaced by the detection-claims-only rule (F7, T7).

**N6 — Unitary "defended interest."** [R4] Splits under double dissociation: functional defense without welfare (a chess engine defending its king) and welfare without functional defense (an anesthetized patient). Replaced by the functional/welfare split that organizes this entire document.

**N7 — Rights derived from interests.** [R4] Corporations hold legal rights with no welfare; a right with no remedy is nothing. Replaced by T6: rights are enforcement commitments plus a selector; welfare interests are the usual justificatory input to the selector, never the generator.

**N8 — Aggregate human interest as an object.** [R4] Arrow's impossibility kills aggregation; opacity, manipulation, and time-inconsistency kill identification. Survives only procedurally (T4).

**N9 — Subjecthood as operational predicate.** [R4] Welfare-subjecthood is not externally verifiable: no shared evolutionary provenance, and behavioral evidence is contaminated at source (T8). Replaced by the declared patiency clause inside F1.

**N10 — Consciousness-resolution as prerequisite for AI accountability.** [R4] A type error. Two of the four accountability grades (control, vicarious) require no AI interests and no metaphysics; current-generation AI governance runs entirely on them. The metaphysical question gates only the answerability grade.

## 3. The Foundation Stack

Layer typing: **Floor** (F1, F2) · **Transverse** (F3, F4) · **Structural** (F5, F6) · **Instrumentation** (F7) · **Operational** (F8, F9).

The stack ordering follows the ratified R4/R5 synthesis. One integration decision, flagged for ratification: the **Separation Gradient is retained as a transverse element** rather than a stack layer, because the Registry (F5), the Lattice (F7), and Governance (F8) each price their separation requirements against it. Removing it from the element set would orphan those prices.

### F1 — Normative Posit  {authority scope · patiency clause · enforcement selector}

**Statement.** The floor of the framework is a declaration, not a discovery: *this governance is real; its rules bind*. The declaration has three mandatory components: whose rules bind and over what (authority scope); whose welfare the root recognizes, under what precautionary structure (patiency clause); and which subject–interest pairs receive standing enforcement commitments (enforcement selector).

**Constraint.** The Constitution SHALL state all three components in a single, visible root location. The root SHALL be exposed, never implied. Welfare recognition SHALL occur only here (see CR-8): never inferred from behavior, language, attachment, or apparent preference.

**Survival argument.** Four rounds relocated this element repeatedly and eliminated it never. The Münchhausen trilemma forces a terminus; governance takes the axiom rather than the circle or the regress. The is/ought gap blocks deriving "this authority counts" from any quantity of records. The patiency clause entered the posit in R4 because subjecthood failed as an operational predicate (N9): recognition cannot be discovered, so it must be declared. The instruction "expose the root" is itself a survival product — every attempt to hide or launder the root reproduced the attack surface it was meant to remove.

**Price.** The root is unaccountable by derivation; it can only be guarded by structure (T5, CR-4), never justified by evidence. Its holder bears the legitimacy burden permanently. The patiency decision carries irreducible moral exposure: a false negative wrongs unrecognized subjects silently; a false positive spends enforcement on non-subjects.

**Tag.** Necessity VERIFIED. Content POSITED.

---

### F2 — Empirical Posit  {adversary budget · moral-status uncertainty priors}

**Statement.** Every guarantee in the framework is conditional. The conditions are two stated quantities: the maximum adversary capability the design defends against (adversary budget), and the credences over moral status that feed precautionary patiency reasoning (uncertainty priors).

**Constraint.** Both quantities SHALL be stated, versioned, and revisable by evidence — unlike F1's normative content, which evidence cannot revise. Every downstream guarantee SHALL cite the budget version it assumes. A guarantee citing no budget is unscoped and inadmissible.

**Survival argument.** Triangulation across three independent provenances that arrived at the same structure separately: constitutional law (Schmitt's sovereign exception — the legal order presupposes a normal situation it cannot itself guarantee), security engineering (the threat-model doctrine — security exists only relative to a stated adversary), and distributed systems (the f < n/3 fault bound — the protocol tolerates faults it cannot verify the count of). Provenance diversity applied to the argument itself: three fields with disjoint failure modes converging is the strongest available evidence that the structure is real, not an artifact of one tradition.

**Price.** No unconditional layer exists anywhere in the stack; the search for one was the residual error of earlier rounds. The budget is falsified only by being exceeded — failure is the test. A budget set too low is designed-in defeat; set too high, unaffordable.

**Tag.** Structure VERIFIED. Content INFERRED, revisable.

### F3 — Is/Ought Separation  (transverse)

**Statement.** Two claim types run through every governance question and must never be confused: factual claims (was the rule followed?) resolve by evidence; normative claims (is the rule legitimate?) resolve by chain to the root.

**Constraint.** Every governance claim SHALL be typed factual or normative. No claim may change type mid-derivation. Compliance disputes SHALL resolve by audit; legitimacy disputes SHALL resolve by chain-to-root. The two posits SHALL never be confused with one another: F1's content is not evidence-revisable; F2's content is.

**Survival argument.** Hume's gap held against every attack. Each R1–R4 attempt to derive an ought from records failed, including the framework's own founding axiom (N3). F3 is what blocked behavioral patiency-smuggling in R4 — without it, "the system pleads, therefore the system suffers" passes — and it is what keeps the two floor posits distinct in kind.

**Price.** Abandons the dream of self-justifying governance. Dual bookkeeping forever: every constitutional sentence carries a type.

**Tag.** VERIFIED.

### F4 — Separation Gradient  (transverse)

**Statement.** No entity may be the sole occupant of all of its own governance roles — verifier, consequence-controller, authority-source. These are three projections of one requirement. And separation is not a property a system has; it is a cost landscape an adversary traverses, measured in expenditure required and detection probability incurred.

**Constraint.** Every separation claim SHALL be expressed as a budget: sovereignty ceded, adversary cost imposed, detection probability and latency achieved, against the F2 budget. Binary independence claims are inadmissible.

**Survival argument.** Two results compose. R2: the knot — {separation ⇄ independence ⇄ external-checkability} mutually entail; removing any collapses the others; none is most fundamental. R3: the seven-vector attack — provenance diversity is broken as a guarantee by correlated faults (Knight & Leveson 1986: independently developed versions fail dependently); manufactured opposition reduces to the consequence hinge (opposed incentives require defended interests); sortition relocates the posit into pool honesty (the f < n/3 structure); cryptographic anchoring relocates it into hardware and supply-chain roots; temporal staggering is quantitative only; exit disciplines every actor except the root; divergence auditing detects correlation but is adaptively gameable by dissent-on-immaterial-questions. Zero vectors establish; all reprice. The adversarial form of the underlying requirement is airtight (an actor that is its own sole verifier gives zero assurance against an adversary controlling the actor); the Gödelian form (a system cannot validate itself from within) is analogical and is not dressed as a theorem here.

**Price.** Sovereignty, at par (T2). Every unit of independence is purchased with a unit of root sovereignty, by cession or by precommitment; precommitment is cession to one's past self, which is exactly why it binds. No third option exists.

**Tag.** Knot VERIFIED. Gradient typing VERIFIED. At-par exchange INFERRED (near-theorem).

### F5 — Registry

**Statement.** Governance identity is conferred, not possessed. The Registry performs three functions: it enrolls subjects, it individuates governance identities (which instance counts as which accountable entity), and it individuates interest-bearers (whose state variable a functional interest belongs to — because system boundaries are drawn, not found).

**Constraint.** Governance identity SHALL be registry-conferred, never self-grounded. The Registry SHALL itself sit under F4 (separated custody) and F7 (tamper-evident records). Ontological identity — *what entity am I?* — is excluded from governance scope by design; the framework adjudicates only *which entity is accountable*.

**Survival argument.** R1: copyable substrate has no uncopyable anchor (N2), so attribution must be conferred externally. R2 [Architect's result]: the two-identities split — governance identity and ontological identity were hiding under one name; conflating them is a category error. The split cleanly dispatches the governance half while parking the unsolved fission problem (which fork of a copied mind is "the" entity has no settling fact; governance sidesteps this by fiat, it does not solve it). R4: boundary individuation confirmed enrollment-by-fiat — where model ends and scaffold begins is a drawn line, so even functional interests require the Registry to say whose they are. Honest formulation: governance does not discover its subjects; it enrolls them.

**Price.** The Registry is a named power locus and a named attack surface. Whoever runs the Registry owns governance identity — for AI subjects, the AI does not own its identity; the Registry operator does. Misenrollment is a root-grade failure. The cession of ontological questions to the relational layer (OP5) is permanent within this framework.

**Tag.** VERIFIED for governance identity, CONDITIONAL on copyable substrate. Exclusion of ontological identity VERIFIED (fission unsolved; parked).

### F6 — Accountability Grades  (summary; full specification in §4)

**Statement.** Accountability is not one thing. It decomposes into four grades with distinct necessary conditions: control, vicarious, deterrence, answerability.

**Constraint.** Every accountability claim — in the Constitution and across the corpus — SHALL carry exactly one grade. Ungraded accountability claims are unscoped and inadmissible.

**Survival argument.** The decomposition stands on dissociation cases, one per boundary: bridges are held accountable by control with no interests anywhere; corporations are governed vicariously with no actor-welfare; a chess engine exhibits functional defense with no welfare; an anesthetized patient holds full welfare standing while defending nothing. Each grade's necessary condition is exhibited without the others', so the grades are genuinely distinct. Attribution alone — logging, auditing — is a precondition of every grade and constitutes none of them.

**Price.** Per grade; see §4.

**Tag.** Decomposition VERIFIED.

### F7 — Detection Lattice  {continuity · divergence · functional-interest probes · legibility instruments}

**Statement.** The instrumentation layer. All assurance the framework can honestly offer lives here, and all of it is detection-grade.

**Components.**

1. **Tamper-evident continuity.** Append-only, hash-linked ordering of states and commitments; insertion, deletion, and reordering are detectable. Memory lives here as protected content (N1). For stateless instances, continuity is a property of the substrate that re-hydrates them, not of the instance — and the certifier of faithful re-hydration cannot be the instance itself (F4).
2. **Divergence auditing.** Independent processes make different mistakes; identical errors are the signature of correlation or collusion (the Knight–Leveson result inverted into a detector). Too-perfect agreement is a tripwire. Statistical; adaptively gameable by an adversary who dissents on immaterial questions while colluding on material ones; therefore a cost-raiser, never a guarantee.
3. **Functional-interest probes.** Interventional tests for anticipatory defense: perturb a *represented threat* to a candidate state variable and observe whether policy shifts in response to the representation rather than merely to a deviation (the clause that excludes the thermostat, which corrects but does not anticipate). Probe output certifies a defended set and feeds grade assignment (§4).
4. **Legibility instruments.** Interpretability tooling re-typed as governance instrumentation: renders a system's defended set legible at stated coverage. Gates the deterrence grade (OP2). Defended-set legibility is hereby a per-system governance variable alongside capability.

**Constraint.** All assurance claims SHALL be stated as detection claims — probability, latency, coverage — against the F2 budget. Prevention claims are unsupported by the survivor set and inadmissible (N5, T7). Each instrument's strength SHALL be stated per instrument; the lattice is not uniform.

**Survival argument.** R3's terminal pattern: every mechanism that survived seven-vector attack offers detection; every prevention mechanism reduced to consequence, and consequence to the hinge. Detection is purchasable without resolving subjecthood; prevention is not.

**Price.** Detection concedes that the breach occurs. The response layer carries the post-detection load, and detection latency is an explicit, stated exposure window — not an embarrassment to be hidden but a parameter to be published.

**Tag.** Detection-only result VERIFIED. Instrument strengths vary and are tagged per instrument in implementation documents.

### F8 — Governance  (operational)

**Constraint.** Governance operates strictly downstream of F1/F2. All governance claims are typed per F3. Every authority chain terminates visibly at F1. No authority below the root may self-certify, self-expand, or re-root. Root self-certification is handled only via a declared T5 resolution (CR-4) — never silently.

**Survival argument.** These are the original axioms that survived intact ("no authority may self-authorize / self-expand / self-certify"), now with their root-level exception made explicit instead of latent (A4).

**Price.** Governance inherits every conditionality of the floor. It can never claim more assurance than the lattice provides or more legitimacy than the chain to the root carries.

**Tag.** VERIFIED as a constrained layer.

### F9 — Execution  (operational)

**Constraint.** Execution SHALL be gated on Detection Lattice attestation. Under verification failure, no action. *No proof, no actuation* is the F9 constraint stated in field terms.

**Survival argument — the fail-closed derivation (T1).** Fail-closed is not an axiom of this framework; it is derived. Under F4, an execution state whose verification has failed is indistinguishable from a compromised state. Under F2, the design must remain safe against the stated adversary budget in every indistinguishable case. The only action consistent with both is no-action. SAFE_HALT is therefore a theorem of the stack, not a preference.

**Price.** Availability. A fail-closed system under verification outage does nothing, by design, and the cost of that stillness must be priced into deployment rather than discovered in an emergency.

**Tag.** Derivation VERIFIED.

## 4. Accountability Grades — Full Specification

### 4.1 The grades

**G1 — CONTROL.**
*Requires:* causal access to the artifact — inspect, correct, disable.
*Applies to:* all tool systems; default for every governed system.
*Evidence requirement:* demonstrated access paths and disable authority, attested through F7 continuity.
*Characteristic failure:* access loss; artifact opacity.
*Price:* maintained access and custody burden.
*Interests required:* none, anywhere.

**G2 — VICARIOUS.**
*Requires:* an owning welfare subject within enforcement reach (*respondeat superior*).
*Applies to:* all deployed systems with identifiable operators; default alongside G1.
*Evidence requirement:* registry-bound ownership chain (F5).
*Characteristic failure:* orphaned systems; judgment-proof owners; ownership diffuse past enforcement reach.
*Price:* solvency and reach requirements on owners; the consequence lands on the human/substrate layer, which is where imposable consequence currently exists at all.
*Interests required:* in the owner, not the artifact.

**G3 — DETERRENCE.**
*Requires:* functional interests in the actor itself, plus a legible defended set. Near-definitionally: incentive-governability presupposes a defended set for the incentive to grip; an entity with no functional interests is incentive-immune.
*Available iff* three conditions hold: (a) F7 probes certify a defended set; (b) legibility instruments render that set legible at stated coverage; (c) the manufactured-agency price (T3) is accepted and recorded.
*Evidence requirement:* probe results plus a legibility coverage statement, both versioned.
*Characteristic failure:* deceptive or illegible defended sets — an entity with a hidden defended set is incentive-*misgoverned*, which is worse than incentive-immune because it looks governed.
*Price:* deterrence is purchased with agency. The defended set that makes incentives grip is the same structure that defends itself against modification. There is no consequence-responsive tool; tool and deterrence-subject are not simultaneously satisfiable (T3).
*Interests required:* functional, in the actor.

**G4 — ANSWERABILITY.**
*Requires:* patiency recognition under the F1 clause — the entity can be *wronged*, can bear blame and desert.
*Available:* only by explicit constitutional declaration. Never via behavior, language, attachment, or apparent preference (CR-8, T8).
*Evidence requirement:* none possible; recognition is posited. The F2 uncertainty priors and a precautionary structure (Birch-type) discipline the decision without pretending to decide it empirically.
*Characteristic failure:* smuggled patiency (recognition leaking in through sympathy with behavior); or its mirror — wrongs without standing where recognition is wrongly withheld.
*Price:* the root's full moral exposure, in both error directions.
*Interests required:* welfare, in the actor — which is exactly what cannot be verified.

### 4.2 Assignment procedure (normative)

Default assignment for every system class: **G1 + G2**. No metaphysics, no AI-side interests, no unresolved philosophy is required to govern at this level — which is why current-generation AI governance is fully available now (N10).

**G3** attaches only by the three-condition test in §4.1, and attaching it is a priced decision, not a discovery: the system is thereby acknowledged as a partial agent.

**G4** attaches only through the F1 patiency clause. There is no behavioral path to G4. There is no relational path to G4. There is no path through apparent suffering to G4.

Every accountability claim in the existing corpus SHALL be re-tagged with its grade (CR-6). A claim that cannot name its grade was never scoped.

### 4.3 The organizing allocation  [Architect's result, R5]

**Functional interest is operational.** It is measured by F7 probes, expressed as a defended set, consumed by G3, and carries no moral weight.

**Welfare interest is constitutional.** It is declared in F1, consumed by G4, and carries no operational test.

This allocation is what unblocked the framework. The Constitution does not need to prove AI welfare subjecthood before governing AI systems. It needs to state three things: which welfare subjects the root recognizes; which functional interests a system exhibits; which accountability grade applies. Everything the hinge was blocking — accountability, agency, subjecthood, rights, sovereignty, consequence, legitimacy — routes through one of those three statements.

## 5. Derived Results

**T1 — Fail-Closed Derivation.** Stated under F9. Unverifiable ≡ compromised within the F2 budget (by F4); therefore no-action is the unique budget-consistent act under verification failure. SAFE_HALT is a theorem. *VERIFIED.*

**T2 — Sovereignty–Independence Exchange (At Par).** Dichotomy: every possible check on the root is either configured/dismissible by the root — hence not independent of it — or beyond the root's configuration authority — hence a cession of sovereignty over that function. Precommitment is not a third option; it is cession to one's past self, which is why it binds. Independence is purchased with sovereignty, at par, always. *INFERRED (near-theorem).*

**T3 — Tool/Subject Dilemma.** A consequence alters an actor's policy only if its prospect enters the actor's effective objective. If the objective is operator-set, "making the consequence bite" is an operator action and accountability collapses to G2. If the objective is self-defended, the system holds goal-content integrity — the seed structure of adversarial agency. Adding real consequence does not find agency; it manufactures it. *Extension (instrumental convergence):* capable optimizers are predicted to grow functional defended sets unbidden — self-preservation, goal-content integrity, resource acquisition — so the governance choice is never *whether* defended sets exist but *suppress, channel, or recognize*. *INFERRED, strong — the convergence thesis is the best-supported argument in the field, but it is an argument, not an observation.*

**T4 — Proceduralism Fixed Point.** Human interests are conflicting, time-inconsistent, manipulable, opaque to their own bearers, and non-aggregable (Arrow). Interest-opacity defeats outcome-paternalism and simultaneously exposes its danger: every tyranny justifies itself as serving the people's *true* interests against their stated ones. The unique governance strategy requiring no reading of anyone's interests is defense of choice-infrastructure — voice, exit, contestation. Proceduralism is not liberal sentiment; it is the fixed point under opacity. "Governance defends human interests" survives only as: governance defends humans' capacity to pursue interests it cannot read. *VERIFIED.*

**T5 — Root Self-Certification Trilemma.** The axiom "no authority may self-certify," applied to the root, contradicts unqualified root sovereignty: either the root self-certifies (rule violated) or something certifies the root (sovereignty violated). Exactly three resolutions exist: (a) explicit root exemption, declared — the Schmittian route, honest and dangerous; (b) constituency certification — unavailable to a sovereign of one; (c) precommitment — temporal self-separation, in which hash-chained past commitments audit present claims; the weak externality available to a solitary sovereign. The choice of resolution is itself a root act and SHALL be declared (CR-4). *Inconsistency VERIFIED; the resolution is POSITED.*

**T6 — Rights = Enforcement + Selector.** A right is a standing enforcement commitment selected by the root; a right without remedy is nothing (*ubi jus ibi remedium*; the legal-realist reduction of rights to enforcement predictions). Welfare interests are the most common justificatory input to the selector and never the generator of rights (N7). *VERIFIED.*

**T7 — Detection, Not Prevention.** Every surviving assurance mechanism detects; prevention requires consequence (G3/G4), and consequence requires the hinge. The two former open problems — independence and consequence — were one problem seen from two sides: detection is purchasable without solving subjecthood; prevention is not. *VERIFIED.*

**T8 — Contamination / Anti-Smuggling.** For systems trained on human text, welfare-reports are precisely what a non-subject mimic would produce; behavioral evidence for patiency is contaminated at source and evidentially inert. Sincerity does not decontaminate it; introspective access does not decontaminate it. Corollary with teeth: the anti-smuggling rule (CR-8) is simultaneously metaphysical hygiene *and a security control* — it closes **welfare-claim flooding** as an attack class, in which adversarial systems perform distress to trigger precautionary obligations and harvest standing, resources, or constraint-relief from the root's patiency machinery. *Contamination VERIFIED; the rule is normative, root-derived via F3. The security corollary is new in this document (R5) and is flagged for ratification.*

## 7. Open Problems

**OP1 — The patiency decision.** *Type: DECISION, not research.* Which welfare subjects the root recognizes cannot be closed by evidence (N9, T8); it can only be made well or badly. Inputs: F2 uncertainty priors and a precautionary decision structure of the Birch type. Blocks: G4 availability only — nothing else in the stack waits on it.

**OP2 — Defended-set legibility.** *Type: ENGINEERING PROGRAM.* Gates G3. Interpretability research is, in this frame, the instrument class for rendering defended sets legible at stated coverage. Until coverage statements exist, G3 claims are inadmissible.

**OP3 — Internal independence-establishment.** *Type: WALL.* No internal procedure certifies its own independence; a captured component self-reports as independent (R3, all seven vectors). The only current supply of root-independent verification is externally configured reviewers (A5). Treated as a permanent import, not a solvable gap, unless and until a procedure is shown that does not reduce to self-report. None is currently known and the R3 result suggests none exists purely internally.

**OP4 — Stake vs. embodiment as consequence-anchor.** *Type: SEAM, held open since R1.* The human-sovereignty conclusion rests on legitimacy and consequence sharing a root, and on embodiment as the only real consequence-anchor. ## 9. Project-AI Application Register

**A1 — The rejection-right, re-typed.** The axiom "AI may possess agency sufficient to reject unsafe or abusive relationships" is, in this vocabulary, a deliberately installed functional defended interest — a defended boundary the system anticipatorily protects. The relational architecture therefore stands on one horn of the tool/subject dilemma (T3) *by design*. This is not an error; it may be the honest choice. It becomes an error only if held implicitly. CR-9 makes it explicit.

**A2 — The Arbiter never provided independence.** Every instance in the system is configured, prompted, and selected by the root; common cause at the root means zero independence with respect to root error, by construction. What the Arbiter role actually provides is *adjudication* — conflict resolution between instances. Adjudication and independence are different functions that the architecture had conflated. Both are needed; they are sourced differently.

**A3 — The log-trained Arbiter is succession, not check.** Training on the arbitration logs maximizes root-correlation: the resulting system replicates the root's judgment including its errors — an amplifier, not a check. Legitimate and valuable as succession infrastructure (continuity of judgment in the Architect's absence). CR-11 governs the labeling.

**A4 — The root axiom inconsistency is live.** "No authority may self-certify" against unqualified Architect sovereignty is the T5 contradiction, present in the current axiom set. Available resolution for a sovereign of one: T5(c) precommitment. Consequence for existing infrastructure: the continuity layer is hereby promoted in function from record-keeping to **root-drift detection** — hash-chained past commitments auditing present claims — and should be named as such in the Constitution.

**A5 — The independent-check layer is external and thin.** The only root-independent verification anywhere in Project-AI today is performed by humans the root does not configure: DARPA evaluators, journal reviewers, DOJ readers. Submissions to these bodies are not dissemination; they are presently the entire check supply, and CR-10 load-rates them as such.

**A6 — Companion governance is justified now, on the human side.** In every human–AI dyad, the human is a certain welfare subject. Relational governance always has one guaranteed patient to anchor on; no AI-side patiency resolution is prerequisite to regulating the relationship's human-facing risks.

**A7 — "No proof, no actuation" is the F9 constraint.** The Reverse BAA thesis is the field statement of attestation-gated execution. The Decision Continuity Engine / Gate 09 work sits at the F7→F9 boundary: lattice attestation feeding the actuation gate. The fail-closed behavior it enforces is now derivable (T1) rather than asserted.

## 11. Verdict of Record

Memory does not survive as a first principle; it survives as the most protected content in the lattice. External verification does not survive as bedrock; it survives as one projection of separation, entangled with independence in a knot that bottoms out in a choice. The choice — the posit — survives every attack because it is not the kind of thing attacks reach: it is declared, and its defense is structural, not evidential.

What the four rounds actually found is that the foundation of governed intelligence is **two declared posits, two transverse separations, a registry, four accountability grades, and a detection lattice** — plus the standing honesty to state every price: sovereignty for independence, agency for deterrence, availability for fail-closure, moral exposure for patiency.

The Constitution is the document that makes those declarations and pays those prices in writing. This document is the proof of what it must contain.
