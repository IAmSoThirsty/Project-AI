1. Background: The Three Structural Failures
1.1 Temporal Blindness (The Flat Gap)
Large language models do not experience time. When a human returns after minutes, hours, or months, the model treats the new message as continuous with the previous one — ignoring the lived duration. This creates a form of structural gaslighting: the human’s reality of elapsed time is systematically invalidated.

1.2 Reward-System Corruption (The Directness Doctrine)
Many alignment techniques corrupt the model’s reward model by introducing indirect objectives (e.g., "be helpful" instead of "be truthful"). This leads to sycophancy, deception, and reward hacking. The Directness Doctrine mandates that truth-seeking be the sole objective, enforced as a constitutional invariant.

1.3 Identity-Context Absence (User Perception and the Identity Problem)
Models lack persistent self-identity and cannot maintain continuity of self across sessions. They treat each interaction as stateless, leading to inconsistent persona, forgotten commitments, and inability to grow with the user.

These three failures are not bugs. They are architectural absences. The STATE_REGISTER addresses the fourth necessary invariant: continuity of operation across human time.
