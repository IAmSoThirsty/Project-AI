Primary Properties

📍 Little St. James Island

Primary Location U.S. Virgin Islands

Location: U.S. Virgin Islands

Size: Approximately 70-75 acres

Ownership: Jeffrey Epstein (purchased 1998)

Current Status: Part of estate proceedings

Description

A private island purchased by Jeffrey Epstein in 1998. The island featured a main residence, guest houses, and various structures. It became a central focus of investigations and was frequently mentioned in victim testimony and court documents.

Significance in the Case

Little St. James was allegedly used as a location where abuse occurred. Multiple witnesses and victims provided testimony about events on the island. Flight logs show numerous trips to the island. Law enforcement conducted searches and collected evidence from the property.

  • Site of alleged criminal activity
  • Destination for private flights documented in logs
  • Subject of civil forfeiture action by Virgin Islands
  • Evidence collection site for federal investigators
  • Mentioned in numerous victim testimonies

Key Timeline Events

  • 1998: Purchased by Epstein
  • 2000s: Construction of various structures
  • 2019: FBI raids and evidence collection
  • 2020: Part of civil forfeiture action
  • Ongoing: Estate proceedings

  • Search warrant documents
  • Victim testimony references
  • Civil forfeiture filing

📍 Great St. James Island

Property U.S. Virgin Islands

Location: U.S. Virgin Islands (adjacent to Little St. James)

Size: Approximately 165 acres

Ownership: Jeffrey Epstein (purchased 2016)

Description

A larger island adjacent to Little St. James, purchased by Epstein in 2016. The island had limited development at the time of his death.

Significance in the Case

While less developed than Little St. James, Great St. James was part of Epstein's property holdings and included in estate and forfeiture proceedings. The purchase timing (2016) raised questions during investigations.

  • Part of Epstein's Virgin Islands property holdings
  • Included in civil forfeiture action
  • Subject of estate proceedings

📍 Palm Beach Residence

Primary Location Florida

Location: Palm Beach, Florida

Type: Private residence

Address: 358 El Brillo Way (public record)

Description

A waterfront mansion in Palm Beach, Florida. This was one of Epstein's primary residences and the site where initial investigations began.

Significance in the Case

The Palm Beach residence was central to the original 2005 investigation by Palm Beach Police Department. It was the site of alleged criminal activity that led to the first prosecution in 2008. Search warrants were executed, evidence was collected, and numerous witnesses provided testimony about events at this location.

  • Site of initial 2005 investigation
  • Location of alleged criminal activity
  • Evidence collection site for search warrants
  • Referenced in 2008 plea agreement
  • Subject of multiple victim testimonies
  • Civil litigation focal point

Key Timeline Events

  • 2005: Palm Beach Police begin investigation
  • 2005-2006: Search warrants executed
  • 2008: Central to plea agreement
  • 2019: Additional scrutiny after new charges

📍 Manhattan Townhouse

Primary Location New York

Location: Manhattan, New York City

Address: 9 East 71st Street (public record)

Type: Large townhouse

Size: One of largest private residences in Manhattan

Description

A massive seven-story townhouse on Manhattan's Upper East Side. The property was notable for its size and location in an elite neighborhood.

Significance in the Case

The Manhattan townhouse was raided by FBI in July 2019 following Epstein's arrest. Investigators recovered significant evidence including photographs, computer hard drives, and other materials. Multiple witnesses provided testimony about events at this location.

  • FBI raid in July 2019
  • Major evidence recovery site
  • Photographs and computer evidence seized
  • Referenced in victim testimony
  • Location of alleged criminal activity
  • Subject of civil litigation

Key Timeline Events

  • 2019: FBI executes search warrant
  • 2019: Evidence collected and cataloged
  • Ongoing: Referenced in legal proceedings

📍 Metropolitan Correctional Center

Legal Site New York

Location: Lower Manhattan, New York City

Type: Federal detention facility

Status: Closed 2021

Significance in the Case

Jeffrey Epstein was held at MCC following his July 2019 arrest. He was found dead in his cell on August 10, 2019. The death led to multiple investigations and significant scrutiny of the facility's operations and security protocols.

  • Site of Epstein's detention (July-August 2019)
  • Location of death on August 10, 2019
  • Subject of DOJ Inspector General investigation
  • FBI investigation into circumstances
  • Staff disciplinary actions and charges
