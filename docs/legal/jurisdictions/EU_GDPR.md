## DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS

Users have the following rights under GDPR:

### Right of Access (Article 15)
- Request confirmation of data processing
- Obtain copy of personal data
- Information about processing purposes, categories, recipients, retention periods

**Exercise:** Email privacy@project-ai.dev with subject "GDPR Access Request"

### Right to Rectification (Article 16)
- Correct inaccurate personal data
- Complete incomplete personal data

**Exercise:** Account settings or email privacy@project-ai.dev

### Right to Erasure / "Right to be Forgotten" (Article 17)
- Request deletion of personal data when:
  - No longer necessary for processing purposes
  - Consent withdrawn (where consent was basis)
  - Objection to processing and no overriding legitimate grounds
  - Data unlawfully processed

**Limitations:** 
- Acceptance ledger entries retained permanently for legal compliance
- Anonymized/pseudonymized data may be retained
- Data necessary for legal claims or compliance not deleted

**Exercise:** Email privacy@project-ai.dev with subject "GDPR Erasure Request"

### Right to Restriction of Processing (Article 18)
- Restrict processing while verifying accuracy
- Restrict instead of erase when needed for legal claims
- Restrict while objection is being considered

**Exercise:** Email privacy@project-ai.dev with subject "GDPR Restriction Request"

### Right to Data Portability (Article 20)
- Receive personal data in structured, machine-readable format (JSON)
- Transmit data to another controller where technically feasible

**Exercise:** Account settings > Export Data or email privacy@project-ai.dev

### Right to Object (Article 21)
- Object to processing based on legitimate interests
- Object to direct marketing (including profiling)
- Absolute right to object to marketing

**Exercise:** Account settings or email privacy@project-ai.dev

### Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making (Article 22)
- Right not to be subject to solely automated decisions with legal/significant effects
- Right to human intervention, explanation, and challenge of automated decisions

**Implementation:** All significant AI decisions subject to human oversight mechanism

## CONSENT MANAGEMENT

**Consent Requirements (Article 7):**
- Freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous
- Clear affirmative action (no pre-ticked boxes)
- Withdrawal as easy as giving consent
- Consent for multiple purposes obtained separately

**Special Categories of Data (Article 9):**
- Explicit consent required for processing sensitive data
- Users warned before entering sensitive information
- Enhanced encryption for special category data

**Children's Data (Article 8):**
- Age verification for users under 16 (or lower age per member state)
- Parental consent required for users under applicable age threshold

## DATA PROTECTION PRINCIPLES

### Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency (Article 5(1)(a))
- Processing documented with lawful basis
- Privacy notice provided at collection
- Clear information about processing activities

### Purpose Limitation (Article 5(1)(b))
- Data collected for specified, explicit, legitimate purposes
- No further processing incompatible with original purpose
- Purpose changes require new consent or lawful basis

### Data Minimization (Article 5(1)(c))
- Only data adequate, relevant, and necessary collected
- Unnecessary data fields removed
- Regular review of data collection practices

### Accuracy (Article 5(1)(d))
- Reasonable steps to ensure data accuracy
- Inaccurate data rectified or erased without delay
- User tools for data correction provided

### Storage Limitation (Article 5(1)(e))
- Data retained only as long as necessary
- Retention periods defined and enforced
- Automated deletion where possible

**Retention Schedule:**
- Active user data: Duration of account plus 90 days
- Acceptance ledger: Permanent (legal obligation)
- Audit logs: 7 years (legal obligation)
- Backup data: 90 days
- Marketing data: Until consent withdrawn

### Integrity and Confidentiality (Article 5(1)(f))
- Appropriate security measures implemented
- Protection against unauthorized processing
- Protection against accidental loss, destruction, or damage
- Regular security testing and updates

## DATA PROTECTION BY DESIGN AND DEFAULT (Article 25)

**Technical Measures:**
- End-to-end encryption for data in transit (TLS 1.3)
- Encryption at rest (AES-256)
- Pseudonymization where appropriate
- Hardware security modules (Enterprise/Government tiers)

**Organizational Measures:**
- Privacy impact assessments for high-risk processing
- Staff training on data protection
- Incident response procedures
- Regular compliance audits

**Default Settings:**
- Minimal data collection by default
- Strictest privacy settings as default
- Opt-in for optional features

## DATA BREACH NOTIFICATION (Articles 33-34)

### Notification to Supervisory Authority (Article 33)
- Within 72 hours of breach awareness
- Contains nature of breach, categories and number of affected individuals, likely consequences, measures taken

### Notification to Data Subjects (Article 34)
- Without undue delay if high risk to rights and freedoms
- Clear and plain language describing breach
- Recommendations for mitigating potential adverse effects

**Contact:** privacy@project-ai.dev

## DATA PROTECTION IMPACT ASSESSMENT (Article 35)

DPIA completed for:
- Systematic and extensive profiling
- Large-scale processing of special categories of data
- Systematic monitoring of publicly accessible areas at large scale

**DPIA Components:**
- Description of processing operations and purposes
- Assessment of necessity and proportionality
- Risk assessment to data subject rights and freedoms
- Mitigation measures
